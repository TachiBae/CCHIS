üíö Postpartum Mental Health Support

It's normal to experience emotional changes - you're not alone

Common Postpartum Emotional Changes

These feelings are normal and experienced by many new mothers

üò¢
Baby Blues
Mood swings, crying spells, and anxiety. Usually appears 2-3 days after birth and lasts up to 2 weeks. This is very common and normal.
üò∞
Anxiety & Worry
Excessive worry about baby's health, feeding, or your ability to care for them. Common in new mothers adjusting to their role.
üò¥
Exhaustion
Physical and emotional fatigue from caring for your newborn, lack of sleep, and recovery from childbirth.
üé≠
Mood Changes
Feeling happy one moment and overwhelmed the next. These fluctuations are caused by hormonal changes after delivery.

When to Seek Help

If feelings of sadness, anxiety, or hopelessness persist beyond 2 weeks, worsen over time, or interfere with caring for yourself or your baby, please reach out for professional support. Postpartum depression is treatable, and help is available.

Where to Get Help / Saan Makakahingi ng Tulong

Barangay Health Worker

Your first point of contact
Visit your local Barangay Health Station for initial consultation and support. They can provide counseling and refer you to specialized care if needed.

Rural Health Unit (RHU)

Professional medical support
Schedule an appointment with a doctor or midwife at your nearest RHU. They can assess your condition and provide treatment or referrals.

Crisis Hotlines

24/7 Support
NCMH Crisis Hotline: 0917-899-USAP (8727)
In-Touch Community: (02) 8893-7603
Free, confidential support anytime.

Remember: Taking Care of Yourself is Taking Care of Your Baby

Your mental health is just as important as your physical health. Don't hesitate to reach out for support - asking for help is a sign of strength, not weakness. You deserve to feel well and enjoy this special time with your baby.
